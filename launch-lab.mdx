Launch Lab is our end-to-end product launch engagement. It combines positioning,
branding, website, product storytelling, and launch assets into one integrated
sprint.

Rather than managing multiple freelancers or agencies, Launch Lab gives startups a
single commercial system ready to launch.

Launch Lab is built around one question:

> **If someone discovers your company tomorrow, will they immediately understand
> why you matter?**

## When to use Launch Lab

Launch Lab is designed for startups approaching an important commercial milestone.
Typical triggers:

- Fundraising or Series A preparation
- Product launch
- New market expansion
- Repositioning
- A new website
- Enterprise sales
- Category creation

## How Launch Lab works

Launch Lab isn't organised by departments — it's organised around decisions. Every
phase exists to remove uncertainty before the next one starts, and every major
decision is approved before production continues. That's how we move quickly
without constantly reopening previous work.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Kickoff">
    A strategic session to define commercial objectives, the launch milestone,
    audience, deliverables, website scope, success criteria, and timeline. We also
    collect everything needed up front — product demo, existing site, pitch deck,
    customer proof, brand assets, analytics, CRM, domain access — so production
    never stops for missing information.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Commercial direction">
    Before we design anything, we align on the commercial story: positioning,
    messaging, customer journey, website architecture, product hierarchy, CTAs,
    and proof strategy. This becomes the blueprint for everything that follows.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Visual direction">
    We explore creative directions — moodboards, brand evolution, typography,
    colour system, UI components, product visualisation. We agree on the visual
    language first; the rest of the design system then follows much faster.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Production">
    With narrative and design approved, we produce the website copy and design,
    product visuals, sales materials, launch assets, and motion. This phase is
    execution, not discussion.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Development">
    Build starts once the design system is stable. Every build includes
    responsive implementation, CMS configuration, technical SEO, performance
    optimisation, forms, analytics, and conversion tracking — a platform your team
    can keep building on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Quality assurance">
    A structured QA pass: responsive behaviour, browser compatibility, forms,
    analytics, CRM routing, SEO, metadata, performance, accessibility basics, CMS,
    redirects, and legal pages. Launch week should be validation, not production.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch">
    The shortest phase — every major decision is already made. Domain migration,
    final QA, tracking validation, form testing, go-live, and monitoring. A good
    launch should feel almost uneventful.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Handover">
    CMS training, a website walkthrough, documentation, access transfer, design
    files, and repository ownership where applicable. The objective is full
    autonomy — see [Handover & ownership](/how-we-work/handover).
  </Step>
</Steps>

Launch Labs generally run between **4 and 8 weeks**, depending on scope.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="4-week">

    launch.

  <Tab title="6-week">

    launch campaign.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="8-week">
    Ideal for larger product launches, enterprise websites, multi-language,
    custom development, or complex stakeholder groups.
  </Tab>

## Scope and technology

Launch Lab operates with a fixed commercial scope. New ideas are welcome
throughout — each one becomes a quick decision: replace an existing deliverable,
move it to a later phase, scope it as an add-on, or include it in a future
[Growth Lab](/growth-lab). More in [Scope & changes](/how-we-work/scope).

Our stack is selected to match the product — typically Next.js, Sanity CMS,
Framer, Vercel, GitHub, and Google Analytics / Tag Manager. See
[Technology](/how-we-work/technology).

## After launch

Many startups continue with [Growth Lab](/growth-lab). Where Launch Lab prepares
the company for launch, Growth Lab focuses on continuous optimization afterwards —
new landing pages, product launches, market expansion, sales enablement, lifecycle
marketing, and growth experiments.

## Common questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do we need Narrative Lab first?">
    Not necessarily. Launch Lab already includes the positioning work required to
    execute the launch. Narrative Lab is ideal when the strategic foundation still
    needs real work.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can we keep expanding the website afterwards?">
    Absolutely. Most Launch Labs are designed with future expansion in mind —
    additional pages can be added over time without rebuilding the platform.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can we manage the website ourselves?">
    Yes. Every Launch Lab is built for client ownership, with training and
    documentation provided at handover.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens if priorities change?">
    Startups evolve quickly. If priorities shift significantly during the project,
    we'll assess the impact together before changing scope — protecting both
    quality and timeline.
  </Accordion>
