## Choosing a Lab

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Which Lab do I need?">
    It depends on your foundation and what you're trying to move.
    [Choosing a Lab](/choosing-a-lab) walks through it, but in short: Narrative
    Lab for strategic clarity, Launch Lab to go to market, Growth Lab to improve
    results after launch.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I need Narrative Lab before Launch Lab?">
    Not necessarily. Launch Lab already includes the positioning work required to
    execute the launch. Narrative Lab is the better start when the strategic
    foundation still needs real work.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I need Launch Lab before Growth Lab?">
    Usually yes. Growth Lab assumes a commercial foundation — positioning,
    messaging, and branding — already exists. Exceptions are uncommon.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I buy a single Lab on its own?">
    Yes. Each Lab is a complete engagement in its own right. Many clients run them
    in sequence, but none of them requires the others.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Working together

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What happens if priorities change mid-project?">
    Startups evolve quickly. If priorities shift significantly, we assess the
    impact together before changing scope. See [Scope & changes](/how-we-work/scope).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do reviews and feedback work?">
    Each phase has a structured review window, and we ask for one primary approver
    to consolidate internal feedback. Details in
    [Feedback & decisions](/how-we-work/feedback).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can we manage everything ourselves afterwards?">
    Yes. Everything is built for client ownership, with training, documentation,
    and access transferred at [handover](/how-we-work/handover).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Why waves instead of a retainer (Growth Lab)?">
    Because startup priorities change quickly. Waves keep us focused on one
    objective at a time while creating natural moments to reassess direction.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
