Launch is a step in the plan, not a scramble at the end. By the time we ship,
everything has been reviewed and tested. What's left is going live, measuring,
and handing off.

## Launch day

<Steps>
  <Step title="Final checks">
    A last pass on performance, links, analytics, and redirects.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go live">
    We deploy to production and confirm everything is working.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Monitor">
    We watch analytics and error tracking closely for the first week.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Handoff

You own everything we build. At handoff you receive:

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="Source and access" icon="key">
    Repository, hosting, and all accounts transferred to your team.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Documentation" icon="book-open">
    How the site is built and how to maintain it after we're gone.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Measurement plan" icon="chart-line">
    The metrics to watch and what good looks like over the first quarter.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Handoff call" icon="phone">
    A walkthrough of everything above with your team, recorded.
  </Card>
</Columns>

<Check>
  The engagement is complete when your team can run everything without us.
</Check>
