Growth Lab is our recurring product marketing engagement. Where [Launch Lab](/launch-lab) prepares a company for launch, Growth Lab supports it afterwards.

Rather than a fixed scope over a predefined timeline, Growth Lab runs in consecutive **waves** — each centred on one business objective.

## When to use Growth Lab

Growth Lab is triggered once you have an initial commercial foundation. Common triggers:

- Product launch completed
- A new funding round
- Expansion into a new market
- Product-market fit becoming visible
- A new ICP or product launch
- Enterprise sales or scaling commercial operations

<Warning>
  Growth Lab assumes positioning, messaging, and branding already exist. If they don't, we generally recommend starting with **[Narrative](/narrative-lab) Lab** or [Launch Lab](/launch-lab).
</Warning>

## How waves work

Every wave is treated as its own project. There is no automatic continuation — each wave focuses on one measurable objective, then earns the next.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prioritise">
    Choose the single commercial priority for the wave.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scope">
    Define the objective, KPI, deliverables, owners, and timeline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Execute">
    Do the work — the disciplines involved depend on the objective.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review">
    Evaluate the KPI outcome, deliverables, and learnings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Decide">
    Continue, pivot, pause, or stop.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### What a wave focuses on

A wave represents one commercial priority — one measurable objective at a time, rather than several unrelated challenges at once. Examples:

- Increase activation
- Improve enterprise conversion
- Launch Germany
- Improve onboarding
- Position a new product
- Support fundraising
- Launch an AI feature

Only **one primary KPI** is selected per wave. Success is evaluated against it during the review.

### Duration

A wave typically lasts **4–8 weeks**, depending on complexity. Longer initiatives are broken into consecutive waves rather than one long roadmap:

```plain text
Wave 1 — Position the enterprise offering
   ↓
Wave 2 — Sales enablement
   ↓
Wave 3 — Outbound campaigns
```

## What's included

Growth Lab is intentionally multidisciplinary. Team composition changes per wave — resources are allocated to the work required, not a fixed team. Depending on the wave, this may involve strategy, product marketing, copywriting, design, development, AI, automation, and research.

Deliverables are determined by the objective. Examples:

### What's not included

Growth Lab is not an unlimited retainer. Outside scope: ongoing social media management, ad spend management, community management, unlimited design requests, and general overflow capacity.

## Commercial model

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Wave-based">
    A predefined scope centred on one business objective. Typical investment **€10–15k per wave**. Best for launches, market expansion, fundraising, and product initiatives.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Multi-wave (most common)">
    Several consecutive waves agreed up front, giving greater continuity while letting priorities evolve between waves. Most clients choose this approach.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

Every continuation is an explicit business decision — Growth Lab never renews automatically.

## Common questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do I need a Launch Lab first?">
    Usually yes — Growth Lab assumes a commercial foundation already exists. Exceptions exist but are uncommon.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can priorities change during a wave?">
    Minor adjustments are expected. Changing the primary objective usually means starting a new wave.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who decides what we work on?">
    Together. Recommendations come from our side, business priorities from yours, and the final scope is agreed before every wave begins.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can we stop after one wave?">
    Yes. Every wave is an independent engagement with no automatic renewal.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why waves instead of retainers?">
    Because startup priorities change quickly. Waves keep us focused on one objective while creating natural moments to reassess direction.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
