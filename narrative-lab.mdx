Narrative Lab is our strategic positioning engagement. It helps startups
articulate what they do, why it matters, and how to communicate it — before
investing in branding, websites, or go-to-market.

Unlike [Launch Lab](/launch-lab), Narrative Lab doesn't end with a website. It
ends with a positioning system your entire team can build on.

## When to use Narrative Lab

Narrative Lab is designed for companies that know **what** they're building but
struggle to explain it consistently. Typical triggers:

- Preparing for fundraising
- Product-market fit starting to emerge
- Founder messaging no longer scales
- A new ICP or market
- The team growing beyond the founders
- Sales conversations feel inconsistent
- The website no longer reflects the product
- Preparing for a Launch Lab

It suits companies preparing a full rebrand just as well as founders who simply
want strategic clarity.

## Format

Narrative Lab is delivered as a **living Notion workspace** — designed to be
referenced continuously by founders, marketers, sales teams, and future agencies,
rather than sitting in static slides or a PDF.

Depending on the engagement, we'll either transfer ownership of the workspace,
duplicate it into your own, or deliver an export. This is agreed before the
project starts.

## How it works

Narrative Lab follows a lightweight process. The objective is clarity, not endless
iteration.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Kickoff">
    A strategic workshop covering your product, founder vision, customer
    conversations, sales process, competition, and market timing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Research & interviews">
    We review the existing commercial landscape — website, sales decks, product,
    competitors, customer reviews. Built on evidence, not assumptions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="V1 narrative">
    The first version of your positioning and narrative.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Feedback">
    A consolidated review round (see below).
  </Step>
  <Step title="V2 narrative">
    A refined version incorporating your feedback.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Final review & playbook">
    A last review, then delivery of the final positioning playbook.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reviews and feedback

Narrative Lab includes **two structured feedback rounds**. Each round should be
consolidated internally before it reaches us, with **one primary approver**
gathering the input — this avoids conflicting feedback arriving at different
moments.

We recommend reviewing the narrative from three perspectives:

<Columns cols={3}>
  <Card title="Strategic" icon="compass">
    Does this accurately reflect the company?
  </Card>
  <Card title="Commercial" icon="trending-up">
    Would this help customers understand the product faster?
  </Card>
  <Card title="Practical" icon="hand">
    Can the team realistically use this day to day?
  </Card>
</Columns>

Not every sentence needs to be perfect. The objective is a system the business can
consistently apply. More on this in [Feedback & decisions](/how-we-work/feedback).

## Scope

Narrative Lab focuses exclusively on strategy and positioning: positioning,
messaging, narrative, copy explorations, and commercial recommendations.

It does **not** include website design, branding, visual identity, development,
or campaign execution — those happen during [Launch Lab](/launch-lab).

## Common questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can Narrative Lab be purchased on its own?">
    Yes. Many founders use it as a standalone strategic exercise before
    implementing the recommendations internally. Others continue into Launch Lab.
    Both are equally valid.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is this a branding project?">
    No. Narrative Lab focuses on strategic foundations. Visual identity, branding,
    and website design are part of Launch Lab.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How many review rounds are included?">
    Two. This encourages clear decision-making while keeping the project focused.
    If substantial strategic changes arise after the second review, we'll discuss
    the best next step together.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Who should be involved?">
    The people who shape the commercial story — typically founders, CEO, head of
    marketing, commercial lead, and product lead where relevant. One person should
    always act as the primary approver.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens afterwards?">
    Three common paths: implement internally using the playbook, continue into
    Launch Lab, or revisit later (for example, before fundraising).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
