Every Lab is built around structured decision moments. Each major phase includes a
review window before we move to the next stage, so work keeps moving instead of
constantly reopening.

## One primary approver

We ask every client to nominate **one primary approver** — the person responsible
for consolidating internal feedback before it reaches us. This keeps
decision-making fast and avoids conflicting input arriving at different moments.

<Tip>
  Gather internal opinions first, resolve disagreements on your side, then send us
  one consolidated direction. It's the single biggest factor in keeping a Lab on
  schedule.
</Tip>

## Reviewing work

When you review a deliverable, three perspectives tend to produce the most useful
feedback:

<Columns cols={3}>
  <Card title="Strategic" icon="compass">
    Does this accurately reflect the company?
  </Card>
  <Card title="Commercial" icon="trending-up">
    Would this help customers understand the product faster?
  </Card>
  <Card title="Practical" icon="hand">
    Can the team realistically use this day to day?
  </Card>
</Columns>

Not every sentence needs to be perfect. The objective is a system the business can
consistently apply — not endless iteration.

## Keeping decisions fast

<Warning>
  Projects slow down most often for one reason: feedback that's scattered or slow.
  Keep feedback for each deliverable in one place, and aim to return decisions
  within the review window so the timeline holds.
</Warning>

The number of formal review rounds depends on the Lab — for example, Narrative Lab
includes two structured rounds. Your addmore lead will confirm the review moments
at kickoff.
