Every Lab operates with a clear commercial scope. That's what makes timelines and
budgets predictable. New ideas are still welcome throughout — they're just handled
as explicit decisions rather than silent additions.

## Handling new requests

New ideas come up in every project. When they do, we'll choose one of four options
together:

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="Replace" icon="repeat">
    Swap it for an existing deliverable of similar effort.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Defer" icon="clock">
    Move it to a later phase in the same engagement.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Add on" icon="plus">
    Scope it as an addition to the current Lab.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Future wave" icon="arrow-right">
    Include it in a future [Growth Lab](/growth-lab) wave.
  </Card>
</Columns>

This keeps the project predictable while still adapting to new opportunities.

## When priorities shift

<Note>
  Startups evolve quickly. If priorities shift significantly during a project,
  we'll assess the impact together before changing scope — protecting both quality
  and timeline.
</Note>

In Growth Lab specifically, changing the primary objective of a wave usually means
starting a new wave rather than reworking the current one. Each wave stays focused
on a single objective.
